Multi selection

A focus group pattern often implemented as a segmented control or segmented buttons is a compact, mostly used as a mutually exclusive selection component that allows users to switch between a small set (typically 2–5) of closely related options with immediate effect.

Its purpose is to enable fast, low friction state changes within the same context such as filtering views, toggling modes, or switching data representations without requiring navigation or form submission.
Unlike checkboxes (multi select) or radio buttons (often form bound), segmented controls are typically action-oriented and update content dynamically, making the currently active option visually and programmatically distinct. The pattern is most appropriate when options are conceptually parallel, of equal weight, and benefit from side by side comparison for rapid cognitive parsing.
